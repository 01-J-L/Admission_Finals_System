Programs Bachelor of Science in Computer Science

Program Overview

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science program at Padre Garcia Polytechnic College is a fully accredited course of study designed to prepare students with a robust and comprehensive understanding of computing principles, software development methodologies, and the theoretical underpinnings of computer science.

Our curriculum reflects the latest industry standards and technological advancements, ensuring graduates are well-equipped with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills necessary for success in the dynamic and ever-evolving technology industry, fostering a solid foundation in critical thinking, analytical reasoning, and ethical responsibility in software development.

Accreditation

ABET accredited program ensuring quality education standards

Industry Partnerships

Collaborations with leading tech companies for internships

Program Objectives

Technical Mastery

To establish a strong foundation in knowledge of mathematical concepts of algorithmic principles that are essential in computer science.

Problem Solving

To develop advanced analytical and critical reasoning skills including and a solid foundation in programming and software engineering.

Innovation Skills

To enable students to specify, design, implement, and test computer software systems, databases, and network infrastructure.

Professional Ethics

To cultivate awareness and skills for identifying problems and addressing effective, efficient, and sound computational solutions.

Core Courses

Our curriculum includes a comprehensive set of core courses designed to build a strong foundation and specialized knowledge. Below is a selection of key courses in the program:

CS 101 3 Credits

Fundamentals in Computing

Introduction to computer systems, binary representation, basic hardware and software concepts, and digital literacy.

Prerequisites: None
CS 102 3 Credits

Fundamentals in Programming (C/C++)

Introduction to programming using C/C++ syntax, semantics, control structures, functions, and debugging.

Prerequisites: CS 101
CSE 101 3 Credits

Living in the IT Era

Overview of information technology's impact on society, ethics in computing, and digital citizenship.

Prerequisites: None
CS 105 4 Credits

Intermediate Programming (e.g., Python/OOP)

Advanced programming concepts including object-oriented programming, data structures, and algorithm implementation.

Prerequisites: CS 102
MATH 201 3 Credits

Discrete Mathematics

Mathematical foundations for computer science including logic, set theory, combinatorics, and graph theory.

Prerequisites: College Algebra
CS 301 4 Credits

Data Structures and Algorithms

Comprehensive study of data structures, algorithm design, complexity analysis, and optimization techniques.

Prerequisites: CS 105, MATH 201
